# Coca-Cola Sales Dashboard — Key HR & Business Insights

## Project Overview
An interactive Excel sales dashboard built for Coca-Cola, analysing regional performance, delivery efficiency, brand profitability, and monthly sales trends across five U.S. regions.

---

## Most Important Insights (Senior HR Manager Perspective)

### 🔴 Workforce & Delivery Performance
- **Average of 37 deliveries per day** across all regions signals a demanding operational tempo — workforce capacity planning and driver scheduling are critical HR concerns.
- Delivery day averages by region and carrier (USPS, UPS, FedEx, DHL) reveal **uneven workload distribution**, suggesting some regional teams may be overstretched.

- **Southeast consistently leads** in both total sales and operating profit — indicating a high-performing regional team deserving of recognition and retention focus.
- **Midwest consistently underperforms** across all metrics — flagging a need for targeted workforce development, sales training, or resource reallocation.

---

### 📅 Seasonal Staffing Signals
- **December and August** are peak sales months across all regions — requiring surge staffing, overtime planning, and logistics support.
- **March records the lowest sales** every month without exception — an opportunity for scheduled training, onboarding cycles, or staff development programmes.

---

### 🏪 Retailer & Brand Intelligence
- **Retailer 1185732 drives 53% of total profit** — over-reliance on a single retail partner is a business continuity risk; account management staffing for this relationship is strategically important.
- **Coca-Cola Classic dominates brand sales** at ~3,989,000 units, followed by Dasani Water — workforce and production resourcing should reflect this brand priority.

---

### 🧠 HR Takeaway
> The data points to a **high-performing Southeast team, a struggling Midwest operation, predictable seasonal demand spikes, and dangerous retailer concentration** — all of which directly inform hiring strategy, training investment, performance management, and workforce distribution decisions.
